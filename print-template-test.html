Volumetric Weight Calculations

Volumetric weight (also known as dimensional weight) is calculated based on the package dimensions. The actual chargeable weight is the higher value between actual weight and volumetric weight.

Formula: Volumetric Weight (Kg) = (Length × Width × Height in cm) ÷ Volumetric Factor

Volumetric Factors by Mode:

  • Air/Premium/Express: Divide by 5000
  • Surface: Divide by 4750
  • LTL (Less Than Truckload): Divide by 4500
  • FTL (Full Truckload): Divide by 4000

Note: For irregular shapes (round, conical, or uneven packages), measurements should be taken at the longest, widest, and highest points, treating the package as a rectangular box for calculation purposes.

Handling and Other Charges

Note: All surcharges mentioned below apply to individual pieces within a consignment. If a single piece/parcel exceeds the specified limits, the surcharge applies only to that particular piece, not the entire consignment.

Heavy Weight Surcharge: Parcels exceeding standard weight limits require special handling and will incur additional charges as follows:

  • Weight over 30 Kg: Additional charge of ₹10 per Kg will be applied.
  • Weight over 50 Kg: Charged double (2×) of its volumetric or actual weight, whichever is higher, due to special handling requirements.
  • Weight over 100 Kg: Charged 2.5 times (2.5×) of its volumetric or actual weight, whichever is higher.
  • Weight over 200 Kg: Rates and additional charges will be discussed and agreed upon on a case-by-case basis for specific consignments, subject to approval.

Heavy packages require additional manpower, specialized equipment, and careful handling procedures to ensure safe transportation. These surcharges cover the extra operational costs involved in moving oversized or heavy shipments.

Over-Length Surcharge: Shipments with any single dimension exceeding standard limits require special handling and transportation arrangements.

  • Length over 1 meter (100 cm): Charged double of its volumetric or actual weight, whichever is higher, with a minimum chargeable weight of 25 Kg.
  • Length over 2 meters (200 cm): Charged triple (3×) of its volumetric or actual weight, whichever is higher, with a minimum chargeable weight of 50 Kg, plus an additional handling charge of ₹2,500 per consignment.
  • Length over 8 feet (244 cm): Rates and additional charges will be discussed and agreed upon on a case-by-case basis for specific consignments, subject to approval.

Over-length shipments cannot be loaded in standard vehicles and require specialized transport, loading equipment, and additional labor, hence the surcharge applies to cover these extra operational requirements.

Important: If a parcel exceeds both weight and length limits (e.g., over 100 Kg and over 200 cm), the highest applicable multiplier will be charged. For example, a parcel over 100 Kg and over 200 cm will be charged 3× of volumetric or actual weight (whichever is higher) plus ₹2,500 handling charge.

Dangerous Goods Regulations (DGR)

Important Notice: Prohibited and Restricted Items

Customer/Consignor/Sender agrees not to ship prohibited and restricted items as per IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations (DGR). If shipped, the sender is fully responsible and may bear penalties enforced by authorities, customs, airlines, or government agencies.

Prohibited Items Include:

  • Explosives and Ammunition
  • Compressed Gases
  • Flammable Liquids and Solids
  • Oxidizers and Peroxides
  • Toxic and Infectious Substances
  • Radioactive Materials
  • Corrosives (Acids/Alkalis)
  • Lithium Batteries (Loose)
  • Perishables and Live Animals
  • Cash, Lottery Tickets, Firearms
  • Alcohol, Drugs, and Narcotics
  • All Liquids and Chemical Powders

The IATA DGR is updated annually and legally enforced worldwide. Staff handling dangerous goods must be trained and compliant. Non-compliance may result in shipment rejection, fines, and safety incidents.
